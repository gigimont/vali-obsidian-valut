## Key Insights

### Newton's ICP range as a calibration anchor (Giuseppe)
Newton Campos's search fund targets companies with 20-100 employees and €10-25M enterprise value. This gives a concrete target range that sits squarely between the Italian micro-businesses (too small) and Lynka (too large at 250 employees / €40-50M). Worth using as a quantitative anchor when defining the ICP.

### Product description anchor: operational blueprint + decision layer (Wolf)
When asked "what are you building?" the cleanest description is two-part: (1) an operational blueprint — a map of how the business works as a network of functionalities, like an Obsidian for the business itself; (2) a decision-making layer — how decisions are made, with what information, and where they can be standardized for delegation. Not a final product description, but a stable anchor point for live prospect conversations. Reference: [[Product Methodology]] from the bootcamp.

### "Similar solutions" framing vs "competitors" (Giuseppe)
Rather than treating other tools purely as competitors to defeat, treat them as similar solutions that have already tried to address adjacent problems. The question becomes: what are they doing wrong, and what does that tell us about how to position? This reframes [[Competitors]] from a defensive document into a learning resource.

### The 20% succession success rate confirmed by a second independent voice
The Colusso Hardware owner volunteered the same number Newton and others have referenced: roughly 20% of his SME peers manage to transfer the business successfully. Independent confirmation of [[Patterns From Fieldwork]] Pattern 19 from a different sector and geography.

### Wolf's overwhelm signal — validation for the audit initiative
Wolf flagged feeling overwhelmed by the volume in Obsidian — not because anything is wrong, but as a felt sense after three weeks of accumulation. This is exactly the trigger Giuseppe's audit/pruning process is designed for: pattern of growth without housekeeping always degrades into noise.

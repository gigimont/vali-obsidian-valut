# Interview 010 — Newton Campos (Campos Equity Partners)

#research #interview #expert #search-fund #investor #buyer-side

> **Date:** May 2026
> **Interviewee:** Newton Campos — Founder/GP of Campos Equity Partners
> **Role:** Search fund investor. Runs a €20M fund based in Spain investing in young entrepreneurs who acquire family businesses.
> **Deal profile:** €10-25M enterprise value, 20-100 employees, 1-6x EBITDA, mostly former family businesses (90% of portfolio)
> **Searcher profile:** 32-42 year olds, often from family business backgrounds, consulting, or investment banking
> **Format:** Video call (transcript available)

## ⚠️ Significance of This Interview

**First direct challenge to the technical feasibility of the core product.** Newton explicitly said "I don't think that's possible" about AI-powered decision replication for SME founders. This is the most important pushback we've received — not because it kills the idea, but because it articulates the objection every sophisticated investor will raise. We need to have a clear answer.

2. Campos provides capital + knowledge to support the acquisition

5. Seller leaves; searcher leads the company toward exit

## What We Learned

### The core objection (must address)
Newton's argument against decision replication is specific and strong:
- AI can only work with the data it receives
- 20-30 years of founder decisions were never recorded
- Even the founder's own children can't replicate the founder's judgment
- Therefore a machine certainly can't

**Why this matters:** This is NOT a dismissal of the problem — he confirms the problem is real ("one of the most challenging things is precisely to replace the former leader"). He's challenging the SOLUTION. His objection is about retroactive extraction of knowledge that was never captured.

**Possible responses (to develop):**
1. We don't claim to fully replicate — we claim to capture enough to reduce the transition risk and compress the handover period
2. Prospective capture (starting now) is more tractable than retroactive capture — his own company proves this ("we record 100% of meetings")
3. The structured interview + AI synthesis approach can extract more than "nothing" even retroactively — the question is how much of the founder's decision logic can be surfaced through deliberate extraction
4. The product might be most valuable when deployed BEFORE the transition (upstream), giving time to build the knowledge base prospectively

### On the search fund model
- €10-25M EV, 20-100 employees, mostly family businesses — overlaps significantly with our ICP
- Searchers are NOT industry experts — they're generalists from consulting/banking/family business backgrounds. This means the knowledge gap at takeover is REAL and structural, not just anecdotal.
- The current knowledge transfer method is a 1-year overlap with the founder — same consulting-bridge pattern from [[Interview 008 — Antonio Rizza (M&A)]] but shorter
- "We only acquire companies that are not very hard to manage" — operational simplicity is a selection criterion, which means even the "easy" companies still have a knowledge transfer problem

### On his counter-proposal: "robot building protocol"
Newton suggested we pivot to: design a protocol for implementing AI automation ("robots") in family businesses post-acquisition. His thesis: "the thousands that survive are the ones that build their robots faster."

**Assessment:** This is a different product than what we're building, but it's adjacent and worth noting. His framing is about operational automation (replacing tasks), not knowledge capture (preserving decisions). Both are real needs. The question is which one we're solving.

### On the market
- His pipeline (4 search funds/week, 2 acquisitions/week) confirms massive deal flow in the family business acquisition space
- 90% family businesses — the succession/transition problem IS the market
- Search fund model is "booming and just going to grow more and more" — the buyer pool for family businesses is expanding

## Patterns Confirmed, Challenged, and New

### Confirmed
- **[[Patterns From Fieldwork]] Pattern 18 — Consulting-agreement bridge** — Campos uses a 1-year founder overlap. Same mechanism, same fragility.
- **The market exists:** 90% of acquired companies are former family businesses. The succession problem is his entire business model.
- **Searchers are generalists, not specialists** — confirms the knowledge gap is structural at takeover

### Challenged
- **Decision replication feasibility** — Newton directly challenges whether AI can retroactively capture founder knowledge that was never recorded. This is the strongest objection we've received and must be addressed in the pitch.

### New

**Pattern 21 — The retroactive vs prospective capture divide**
Newton's own company records 100% of meetings — proving he values knowledge capture. His objection is specifically about doing it retroactively for 20-30 years of unrecorded decisions. This suggests the product may need TWO modes: (a) prospective capture (start recording/extracting now for future use) and (b) retroactive extraction (structured interviews to surface what can still be recovered). Mode (a) is uncontroversial. Mode (b) is where the skepticism lives. The honest answer is probably: retroactive capture gets you 60-70% of the decision logic, not 100%. The question is whether 60-70% is enough to reduce the transition risk meaningfully.
- **Source:** [[Interview 010 — Newton Campos (Search Fund)]]
- **Strength:** Strong — comes from someone who has seen dozens of transitions and does prospective capture himself

## Strategic Implications

### For the pitch
- Must have a clear answer to "but the data doesn't exist" objection
- The answer is probably: "you're right that we can't get 100%. But we can get enough to reduce the 1-year handover to X months and reduce the risk of the transition failing. That saves you Y in consulting fees and Z in deal risk."
- Wolf's personal experience is the proof point: "I spent 1.5 years manually extracting knowledge from my family business. With AI tools, we believe this can be done in weeks, not years."

### For product direction
- Newton's "robot building protocol" suggestion is worth noting but is NOT our product — it's operational automation, not knowledge capture. Different problem, different buyer.
- The prospective vs retroactive distinction should be acknowledged explicitly in our positioning. We're strongest when the founder is still involved (upstream). We're weaker (but still useful) when the founder has already left (retroactive).

### For distribution
- Search funds are a potential distribution channel: if Campos' searchers could use our tool during the 1-year overlap period, it would compress and de-risk the knowledge transfer. Newton might not buy the product for himself — but his searchers might.
- The fund sees 4 search fund applications/week and 2 acquisition opportunities/week — enormous pipeline if we could integrate

## What We Presented

We walked Mary through the current state of the project:

- **Core hypothesis:** the knowledge trapped in founders' and senior employees' heads is now more reachable because of developments in AI — it can be mapped and leveraged to ensure future prosperity
- **Inspired by [[Interview 002 - Ex-Banker, BoD of Deutsche Bank, SMEs view]]**, we framed the direction as building a "mini-Celonis" — process mining adapted for SMEs
- **Value we think we can generate:** smoother generational succession, foundation for digitalization, succession of expertise, preparation for M&A, and consulting
- **How:** a service-as-a-software to shadow the experts (CEO, senior employees), document the status quo, and offer actions
- **Key assumption presented:** organically grown SMEs with informally documented processes will suffer, if not fail, if they do not invest in succeeding their expertise — mainly because experts retire at a faster rate than they can be replaced

## The Clonable Discovery (pivot trigger)

Mary shared an article about **Clonable**, a US startup working in a similar space. Researching Clonable caused a meaningful shift in how we think about the product:

### Before Clonable

Our framing: map processes, document the status quo, create an operational report. Essentially a documentation and process-mining play.

### After Clonable

New framing: use AI to **map, document, AND replicate the decision-making expertise** itself. Not just "here's how the business runs" but "here's how the expert thinks, and here's a system that can imitate those decisions."

### What this means concretely

- We're not just documenting processes — we're **cloning judgment**
- The output isn't just a report — it's a **scalable knowledge asset** that can answer questions and support decisions after the expert is gone
- The technical core shifts from process mapping to **expert imitation / decision replication**
- Framing: "we essentially imitate the processes that happen within their heads in order to make decisions that only these experts can make"

### How this connects to existing thinking

- Aligns with the "knowledge management system" concept from [[Interview 004 - Prisma Founder (Digital Agency, Romania)]]
- Goes further than [[Interview 002 - Ex-Banker, BoD of Deutsche Bank, SMEs view]]'s process mining framing — this is process mining + decision cloning
- Validates the technical bet in [[Problem Statement]] — AI making tacit knowledge extractable
- The [[Council Verdict]] First Principles advisor asked "which of the four problems?" — this answer is firmly **Problem 2 (Readiness)** from [[Four Sub-Problems]], but with a sharper technical edge

## Patterns This Touches

- **[[Patterns From Fieldwork]] Pattern 5 — The irreplaceability myth is load-bearing:** Clonable's model directly attacks irreplaceability.

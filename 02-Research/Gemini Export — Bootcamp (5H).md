# gemini response

It is completely normal that you haven't seen the word "productized" before—it is a specific piece of startup and agency jargon, but understanding it will be a superpower for your pitch. 

To understand a **Tech-Enabled Productized Service**, it is easiest to break it down into its three parts, starting with the word "Productized."

### 1. What does "Productized" mean?
Normally, business offerings are split into two categories: **Products** and **Services**.

* **A Traditional Service:** You hire a consultant. They charge you €200 an hour. The scope of work is custom, the timeline is vague ("it takes as long as it takes"), and the final price is unpredictable. 
* **A Product:** You buy Microsoft Word. It costs a fixed €150. You know exactly what features you are getting, and it works the exact same way for everyone who buys it.

**To "Productize" a service** means taking a human-delivered service and packaging it to sell exactly like a product off a shelf. 
* It has a **fixed price** (e.g., "€5,000").
* It has a **fixed scope** (e.g., "You get exactly these 3 dashboards and 1 SOP document").
* It has a **fixed timeline** (e.g., "Delivered in exactly 14 days").

The customer isn't buying your "time" or your "consulting hours"; they are buying a guaranteed, standardized outcome. 

### 2. What makes it "Tech-Enabled"?

# Interview 004 — Prisma Founder (Digital Agency, Romania)

> **Date:** April 2026 **Interviewee:** Founder of Prisma, a 25-person digital agency in Romania **Role:** Agency founder serving 200+ SMEs over 7 years, DACH market experience **Sector:** Digital marketing, web development, AI implementation for SMEs **Geography:** Romania-based, serving Germany/Austria/Switzerland **Connection:** Via Balash (mutual contact)

## Context

Prisma is a digital agency that helps SMEs enter the digital world — marketing, websites, automation. The founder has 7 years of direct experience working with 200+ SMEs, many of them traditional businesses making their first digital steps. He studied in Vienna, speaks German, and actively works with DACH-market clients. This is an expert interview, not a target-customer interview — but the pattern recognition from 200+ client engagements is extremely valuable.

## What We Learned

### About the product and framing

- He immediately named our concept "digital due diligence" — this label resonated more naturally than anything we've used. Worth testing as a product name.
- The "neutral mirror" framing landed well: "we're not telling you how to do your business better, we're presenting what you already know in clear documentation"
- He validated that the due diligence report IS valuable as a standalone deliverable — it puts the SME owner in a better negotiation position for any future scenario (sale, succession, digitalization)
- He used the term **"knowledge management system"** unprompted — and has built one with a partner in Wiesbaden for larger companies. Validates the technical category.

### About willingness to pay

- **Critical insight:** SMEs won't pay for pure strategy/planning. They need to see tangible deliverables.
- **Recommended model:** Run due diligence AND small implementation in parallel. Two tracks, not sequential.
- **Alternative pricing:** Bundle the due diligence cost into monthly implementation fees — they don't pay separately for the diagnostic, it's included in the ongoing service.
- **Price range discussed:** €5,000–10,000 for the due diligence report was mentioned without resistance. Positioned against "consulting boutiques that charge way more over a longer period."
- **His belief:** "In Germany it should work" — but warned against assuming Germans will pay more just because they're German. Need tangible output regardless.

### About ICP

- **Best target industries:** Manufacturing, logistics — "companies that until now didn't really have to do anything with digitalization"
- **Sweet spot company:** €5–20M revenue, 25–50 employees
- **Sweet spot CEO:** Open-minded, doesn't need to understand everything, but knows something is changing
- **Critical new detail:** Needs **one internal person responsible for digitalization** who can be the contact point. Without this person, nothing sticks.
- **Two failure modes he sees constantly:**
    1. No responsible internal person to manage the digital side → nothing gets maintained
    2. CEO doesn't understand that digital is a long-term investment, not a one-time purchase → expects a website to "change the world" for €2,000

### About the generational dynamic

- **Old entrepreneurs (30+ years):** "Don't tell me what to do." Have money and processes worth optimizing, but resist change. Hardest to sell to.
- **Gen Z entrepreneurs:** Understand digital is necessary, but don't have processes or money yet.
- **Sweet spot (confirmed):** Established companies where the CEO has an open mind. Not necessarily the youngest generation — just the ones who sense the shift.
- **On succession specifically:** He confirmed that the generational handover is "always a topic" and that founders worry about their processes and knowledge being lost. He called it "100% the thing" when we described the knowledge-transfer angle.

### About potential collaboration

- Prisma has 25 specialists (Google Ads, Meta, designers, AI, web dev) based in Romania = lower cost
- They have purchased databases of 50,000+ SME contacts for cold outreach
- Founder explicitly offered to invest or collaborate
- Romania/DACH cost arbitrage: deliver German-quality work at Romanian prices
- **For later:** once our product is more concrete, Prisma could be the implementation layer (they do the digital transformation, we do the knowledge capture/due diligence). Natural partnership.

## New Patterns to Add to [[Patterns From Fieldwork]]

### 7. The internal champion requirement

From 200+ client engagements: nothing works without one internal person who owns the digital transformation. The CEO can be open-minded but if there's no operational owner inside the company, every initiative dies. This has implications for our product: we might need to either (a) require this person exists before engaging, or (b) help create the role as part of our service.

### 8. Parallel delivery beats sequential

SME owners need to see something tangible while the diagnostic is happening. Pure planning → pure implementation is too slow and too abstract. The winning model is: start the due diligence AND deliver a small quick win simultaneously. This keeps the CEO engaged and paying.

### 9. The "neutral mirror" sells better than "improvement plan"

Framing the deliverable as "we wrote down how your business works" rather than "here's what you should fix" avoids triggering the "don't tell me what to do" reflex. The improvements become obvious once the documentation exists — you don't have to name them. This aligns with Wolf's instinct from the pitch transcript.

# Interview 001 — Successor

#research #interview #successor

> **Date:** April 2026
> **Interviewee:** Potential successor (second child) of founder-led SME
> **Context:** Sibling already working 100% full-time in the business
> **Sector:** Industrial / workwear-related

---

## The Business Today

### What's working
- Financially healthy — strong liquidity, good cash reserves
- Real competitive advantage: high margins from Asian supplier relationships (30+ years)
- Founder built from zero, wasn't even in this sector before starting

### What's constraining it
- Operating model is slow — decision throughput limited by one person
- Everything non-standard routes to the founder
- **Zero delegation** — not policy, lived reality
- Employee tacit knowledge also trapped — long-tenured workers can't articulate how they do their job

---

## The Successor's View

### What they see
- Model is fragile precisely because it's profitable
- Strategic opportunities exist that can't be captured under current operating capacity
- Founder-centric model is the ceiling, not the foundation

### Growth opportunities identified
- Public-sector tenders (appalti pubblici) — higher margins
- Disintermediation — cut second-line resellers
- Brand recognition — strong lever in this sector

### Why they can't act
- **No bandwidth** — sibling already at full capacity just running business-as-is
- **No authority** — strategic decisions still route to founder
- **No playbook** — business was never designed to be delegated or systematized

### Core mental model
> "We see the pain points. We know what should change. But we can't get out of the present long enough to build the future — and the person who could give us that runway doesn't believe it's needed."

---

## Patterns Identified

1. **Profitability is the enemy of transformation** — no burning platform
2. **Knowledge trapped at two levels** — founder AND long-tenured employees
3. **Successor's dilemma** — awareness, authority, and bandwidth are in different hands
4. **Growth ambitions vs operational tyranny** — every hour running current model consumes resources for the new one
5. **Standardization paradox** — the valuable stuff is precisely what's considered un-standardizable
6. **Irreplaceability myth is load-bearing** — the entire organization is built around this belief

---

## What This Shifted

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Founder aging out | Years before handover, during peak health |
| Succession is emotional and hard | Next gen inherits undocumented operating knowledge |
| Aging founders are the customer | Next-gen successors (28-45) are the customer |
| Accountants and M&A advisors are alternatives | Consultants (too expensive), doing nothing (the default) |

# Feedback 001 — Carl (Ex-Banker)

#research #feedback #expert #banking

> **Date:** April 2026 **From:** Carl (K.) — ex-banking background **Format:** Written feedback (not a live interview) **Context:** Sent him the project description, received structured written response

---

## His Overall Read

**Validated the problem as real and longstanding.** His opening line: _"The problem you both describe is real and has been an issue forever."_ — but immediately followed with the qualifier that finding a _manageable and affordable_ way to solve it would be the breakthrough. He's not questioning whether the problem exists. He's questioning whether it can be solved at a price SMEs will pay.

Also closed with: _"would be a great step forward in transferring and preserving valuable knowledge in small companies, that — much more than large companies — cannot afford to lose know-how"_ — this frames SMEs as MORE vulnerable than large companies to knowledge loss, which is a useful positioning angle.

---

## Competitive Landscape He Flagged

Carl named specific companies and categories we need to research:

### Process Mining (enterprise-grade)

- **Celonis** — pure process mining, major player
- **Fluxicon** — pure process mining

### Process Intelligence / Automation

- **Workfellow** — process intelligence + automation

### Mid-cap / Mid-market Tools

- **Lucidchart** — process mapping and visualization
- **Kissflow** — workflow and process automation
- **Adonis** — business process management

### His honest assessment

> "I am unable to tell you in how far they are realistically applicable for SMEs"

and

> "I don't know what exactly they offer"

**Key implication:** even someone from banking who knows this space can't tell you whether these tools work for SMEs. That gap in knowledge IS the market gap. If an informed ex-banker doesn't know whether Celonis scales down to a 30-person manufacturing company, the SME owner certainly doesn't know either.

---

## His Strategic Recommendation

Carl gave a clear framework for the next iteration of the project proposal. This is essentially a competitive analysis + positioning brief:

1. **What products/companies exist?**
2. **What do they do and what are they really good at?**
3. **Where is the gap — why is their offering not transferable to smaller companies?**
4. **What would it take to either transfer existing products to smaller customers OR create a new offering?**
5. **Why do you think YOUR offering can fill this gap?**
6. **What does it take to realize this?**
7. **What are the main roadblocks and how can they be overcome?**

### Assessment of this framework

This is classic banking/investor due diligence thinking — and it's the right framework for a pitch deck or investment memo, but it's premature for where we are right now. We're still validating the problem with actual SME owners (see [[1 to 1s — Week 3]] — "all signals so far are second-hand"). The competitive analysis becomes urgent once we've confirmed willingness-to-pay from founders. For now, file it as the homework for weeks 5-6.

## Critical Decision He Surfaced

> "I think you need to consider narrowing down whether it's more mapping and documentation of processes or whether you want to take the next step to automate/optimize (probably the former in order not to increase complexity)"

**This is important.** He's telling us to pick: are we a _documentation_ product or an _automation_ product? His instinct (and ours) is documentation first. This aligns with:

- The [[Problem Statement]] — "document how the business actually runs"
- The "neutral mirror" framing from [[Interview 004 - Prisma Founder (Digital Agency, Romania)]]
- The Executor's advice in [[Council Verdict]] — "broker one deal manually before building software"

The temptation will be to promise automation because it sounds more valuable. Carl is warning us not to. Document first. Automate later. One thing at a time.

## The Sustainability Bootcamp (Spring 2026)

The concept originated at ESMT Berlin during a **sustainability bootcamp** — a separate course from the Vali Hub incubation program. Wolf was part of a 5-person team competing in a 2-day hackathon format. The startup was called **5H**.

### The 5H Team

- **Wolf** (Poland / Germany) — 2.5 years as digital transformation manager inside FILIGRAN, his family's steel manufacturing business in Poland. Knew firsthand how hard it is to extract and formalize tacit SME knowledge without AI tools to help.
- **Balazs** (Hungary / Romania) — CEE marketing expertise. Deep understanding of why traditional SME owners in Central/Eastern Europe reject "AI hype" and need concrete, no-bullshit value delivered differently from Western tech.
- **Jo Máté** (Hungary) — Real estate family business background. Access to a family-business sandbox for piloting.
- **Yannick** (Germany) — Work clothing manufacturing family business. A second sandbox.
- **Francesco Candi** (Italy) — Italian market perspective and finance background. Italy as a primary target market (succession = #1 cause of corporate bankruptcy).

### The Unfair Advantage

Three team members had family businesses ready as zero-acquisition-cost MVP sandboxes: Wolf (steel manufacturing, Poland), Jo Máté (real estate), Yannick (work clothing manufacturing). This meant the team could generate the first case studies without any customer acquisition effort.

## What the Bootcamp Produced

Over two days, the 5H team:

- Assembled country-level succession market data from EU, German, Italian, Polish, Hungarian, and Romanian sources (see [[Market Data]])
- Articulated the three-pillar extraction methodology: Digital Archaeology + Passive Shadowing + Conversational Extraction (see [[Product Methodology]])
- Developed the first revenue model analysis: break-even + upsell, B2B2B broker channel, due diligence premium (see [[Business Model]])
- Built a high-fidelity MVP prototype ("LegacyShift") — a React / Next.js landing page with an interactive organizational report simulator using a 3-level drill-down UI (org chart → employee → task)
- Identified Celonis as the primary competitive reference and articulated the key differentiation: process mining requires digital event logs; SMEs don't have them (see [[Competitors]])
- Prepared a full pitch for judges Joanna Radeke and Jan Zawadzki
- Competed for selection to **INNOVA Europe** (Finland) to represent ESMT

### The Core Strategic Clarity That Emerged

Two realizations came out of the bootcamp that have held through all subsequent fieldwork:

1. **The Neutral Mirror philosophy** — don't sell "AI" or "digitalization" to founders who are allergic to those words. Sell "transparency" and "protection of their legacy and valuation." Don't tell a 60-year-old founder what he's doing wrong. Just document what he's doing right.

2. **The Celonis gap** — traditional process mining tools require digital event logs that organically grown SMEs don't have. Their knowledge lives in people's heads, paper notebooks, and WhatsApp groups. This gap is the entire market opportunity.

## From 5H to the Vali Hub Project

After the bootcamp, Wolf continued developing the concept through the **ESMT Vali Hub incubation program** with a new co-founder: **Giuseppe**, who brings a banking background and deep roots in the Italian SME ecosystem (two different vantage points on the same structural gap).

The core thesis carried forward intact. Key differences in the Vali Hub phase:

- **Focus sharpened:** Germany and Italy (vs. the broader CEE sweep from the bootcamp)
- **ICP refined:** the **successor** (not the founder) is the primary buyer — a shift driven by [[Interview 001 — Successor]]
- **Methodology:** validated through fieldwork (5 interviews with founders, experts, and successors) rather than desk research
- **Team:** Wolf + Giuseppe (2 people, focused) vs. the 5H bootcamp team (5 people, broad)

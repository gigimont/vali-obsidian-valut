# Upstream vs Downstream

#strategy #unresolved #critical

> **Status:** UNRESOLVED — carry both hypotheses into fieldwork

---

## The Question

Do we serve founders **at the moment of succession** (downstream, age 65-75) or **10-15 years earlier** with a readiness product (upstream, age 50-60)?

## Downstream (succession moment)
- Founder is 65-75, actively thinking about stepping back
- Emotional, urgent, high-stakes
- **Problem:** founder is a terrible customer (no urgency until crisis, emotional, resists paying)
- **Sales cycle:** long, unpredictable, often stalls

## Upstream (readiness product)
- Founder is 50-60, business is thriving
- Sold as "build a business that runs without you" (appealing for reasons beyond succession)
- **Better economics:** clearer ROI, shorter sales cycle, less emotional resistance
- **Creates own pipeline:** succession-ready businesses 5-10 years later
- **Problem:** no felt urgency. "Why fix what isn't broken?"

## What First Interview Suggests

[[Interview 001 — Successor]] found a **thriving business with no crisis** where the successor sees the trap. This is the upstream scenario — the problem exists years before handover but nobody frames it as a succession issue yet.

## Resolution Method

**Do not resolve by argument. Resolve by data.** Interview both populations:
- Founders ready to sell/hand over now (downstream)
- Founders 5-10 years from needing to, with successors entering the business (upstream)

Compare: pain level, willingness-to-pay, competitive landscape.

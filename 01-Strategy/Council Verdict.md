# Council Verdict

#strategy #council #analysis 

> **Date:** April 2026
> **Method:** 5 advisors → anonymized peer review → chairman synthesis
> **Question:** How should we define the problem statement for an SME succession solution?

---

## Headline Verdict

**Don't fill in the 5-slot framework yet.** Four of five advisors converged: "SME succession" as currently scoped is too broad. Framework-filling is downstream of wedge selection.

---

## Where the Council Agrees (high confidence)

1. The 5-slot framework cannot be meaningfully filled in yet — "SME succession" is at least [[Four Sub-Problems]]
2. Fieldwork must precede framework — 30 interviews, 10 advisor interviews, one manually brokered deal
3. Current framing is linguistically and emotionally mismatched — "baby creature" vs "SME succession solution"

## Where the Council Clashes

1. **Market size:** Contrarian (saleable market is small fraction of wave) vs Expansionist (€1T+ infrastructure prize). Resolution: both right on different timescales.
2. **How to narrow:** First Principles (pick one of four sub-problems) vs Executor (just interview, let wedge emerge). Resolution: design interviews around the four-problem hypothesis.
3. **[[Upstream vs Downstream]]:** Serve 70-year-olds at handover, or 50-somethings building a business that runs without them? **Biggest unresolved question.**

## Blind Spots Caught in Peer Review

- The **buyer side** is missing — search funders, strategic acquirers, next-gen family buyers have pain too
- **Regulatory/tax specificity per country** — Art. 2112, inheritance tax regimes shape deal structures
- **Employees and community** — founders kill deals that harm workers
- **Family dynamics** — "who in the family wants it" kills deals before external buyers are considered
- **Founder-market fit of the team** — relationship-heavy space, need networks

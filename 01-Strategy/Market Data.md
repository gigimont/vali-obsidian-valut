## EU-Wide: The Scale of the Problem

- SMEs represent **99% of all businesses** in the EU
- They employ roughly **100 million people** — two-thirds of all private-sector jobs
- They generate **56–58% of Europe's GDP**
- Source: [European Commission: Entrepreneurship and SMEs](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/smes_en)

### The Business Transfer Crisis

- Approximately **450,000–610,000 SMEs** change hands every year in Europe, affecting over **2 million jobs**
- Europe loses approximately **150,000 firms and 600,000 jobs every year** exclusively due to inefficiencies and failures in the business transfer process (trapped knowledge, bad handovers)
- A successfully transferred business saves an average of **5 jobs** vs. 2 jobs created by a brand-new startup — transferring existing businesses is more economically efficient than founding new ones
- Source: [European Commission: Business Transfers — Continuity and Exit](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/smes/continuity-and-exit/business-transfers_en)

## Germany

- By end of 2026, approximately **560,000 companies** face management and ownership succession
- More than **57%** of SME owners are now 55 or older
- A major hurdle cited by **45%** of companies: complexity of the handover — directly validates the knowledge-standardization thesis
- Source: [KfW: Nachfolge im deutschen Mittelstand](https://www.kfw.de/%C3%9Cber-die-KfW/KfW-Research/Nachfolge-im-deutschen-Mittelstand.html)
- Press release (Jan 2026): [KfW: Immer mehr Mittelständler wollen nach altersbedingtem Rückzug schließen](https://www.kfw.de/%C3%9Cber-die-KfW/Newsroom/Aktuelles/Pressemitteilungen-Details_876352.html)

### AI Adoption in German SMEs (IfM Bonn, March 2026)

- **1 in 4** German SMEs now use AI (up from 19%; vs. 57% at large corporations)
- Primary barriers: inadequate data infrastructures, poor data quality, unclear data protection requirements
- Source (English): [IfM Bonn: SMEs in Germany are becoming more digital and AI-friendly](https://www.ifm-bonn.org/en/news/meldung/smes-in-germany-are-becoming-more-digital-and-ai-friendly)

## Italy

- Over **85%** of Italian SMEs are family-run, employing roughly **90% of the workforce**
- **~43%** of business leaders are over 60; nearly **30%** are over 65
- Succession is the **#1 cause of corporate bankruptcy** in Italy (AIDAF / Intesa Sanpaolo)
- Survival rates: only **25–30%** of SMEs survive to the 2nd generation; ~15% to the 3rd; ~3–4% to the 4th
- Explicitly cited causes: "unexpressed knowledge" and lack of modern governance
- Source: [Intesa Sanpaolo: Il passaggio generazionale nelle imprese manifatturiere italiane (PDF)](https://group.intesasanpaolo.com/content/dam/portalgroup/repository-documenti/research/it/collana-ricerche/CR_novembre2020.pdf)

## Poland

- **92%** of all SMEs in Poland have family foundations (PARP / COSME)
- Only **5%** of companies successfully achieve a full generational succession where the next generation takes over and the company survives long-term — the single most striking data point
- Context: the first generation of post-communist entrepreneurs (founded businesses in the early 1990s) are now in their 60s and 70s
- Source: [IBR Polska: Model 5 Poziomów Rodzinności Przedsiębiorstw](https://familybusiness.ibrpolska.pl/model-5-poziomow-rodzinnosci-przedsiebiorstw/)

## Hungary

- Between **58–75%** of Hungarian SMEs are family businesses (BGE / Budapest LAB)
- Majority are not prepared for handover — "neither with a strategy nor in terms of corporate and psychological matters" (BGE)
- In 2023: **37,800 business closures vs. 25,800 creations** — major driver is aging X and Baby Boomer founders failing to formalize and pass on their businesses
- Source: [BGE: Sokan vannak, de mennyien lesznek a családi cégek?](https://uni-bge.hu/hu/sokan-vannak-de-mennyien-lesznek-a-csaladi-cegek)

## Romania

- The **"Founder's Trap"** — power, operational knowledge, and decision-making are almost entirely centralized in the founder
- Because knowledge is never standardized into a usable structure, the business literally cannot function if the founder steps away
- Source: [VirtualBoard: Depășirea provocărilor legate de succesiunea afacerilor de familie](https://virtualboard.ro/transferul-afacerilor-de-familie/depasirea-provocarilor-legate-de-succesiunea-afacerilor-de-familie/)

## Why Operations is the M&A Bottleneck

Financials are standardized by accountants. Legal is standardized by lawyers. Operations are highly organic, informal, and undocumented. Buyers slash valuations or walk away entirely when they cannot verify how the business actually runs.

- Source: [DueDilio: Ultimate Guide to Operational Due Diligence](https://www.duedilio.com/operational-due-diligence/)

## The Pitch Hook

> "By the end of 2026, 560,000 German SMEs face succession. In Italy, Poland, and Hungary alone, over 70% of the economy is driven by family SMEs. Yet data shows 70–95% of these companies will die during a generational transfer. Why? Because their operational infrastructure exists entirely in the aging founder's head."

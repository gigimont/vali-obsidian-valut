# Four Sub-Problems

#strategy #framework

> Source: First Principles Thinker in [[Council Verdict]]

---

"SME succession" is not one problem. It is four problems wearing the same coat. Each has different buyers, willingness-to-pay, sales cycles, and competitors.

## 1. Matching
Finding a buyer who fits. Marketplace dynamics.
- **Competitors:** M&A brokers, BizBuySell-style platforms, search funds
- **Buyer:** Seller (founder) or buyer (acquirer)

## 2. Readiness
The business isn't transferable. The founder IS the business. Operational/systemization problem.
- **Competitors:** Management consultants, EOS/Scaling Up coaches
- **Buyer:** Founder or successor
- **This is where our first interview pointed us** → see [[Interview 001 — Successor]]

## 3. Emotional Transition
The founder can't let go. Psychological.
- **Competitors:** Executive coaches, family-business advisors, therapists
- **Buyer:** Founder, sometimes family

## 4. Deal Structuring
Legal, tax, financing.
- **Competitors:** Lawyers, accountants, banks
- **Buyer:** Both sides of the deal

---

## Our Current Hypothesis

We are investigating **Problem 2 — Readiness** as the primary wedge, with the successor (not the founder) as the buyer. The [[Interview 001 — Successor]] strongly pointed here.

The upstream reframe from [[Upstream vs Downstream]] suggests readiness might be the entry point for all four problems — a readiness product for founders in their 50s creates a pipeline of succession-ready businesses for the other three problems later.

---

## Key Question for Next Interviews

Which of these four problems is most painful AND most willing-to-pay? Design interview questions that discriminate between them.

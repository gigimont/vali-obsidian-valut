## Current Thesis (v3 — post-interview)

> Organically grown SMEs are running on expertise that was never written down, and as the people who hold it retire faster than they can be replaced, the businesses inherit a quiet operational debt that AI now lets us repay before it comes due.

### Four-sentence version (for pitches)

Most successful SMEs were built organically, with processes that live in people's heads rather than in documents. As the founders and senior employees who hold that expertise approach retirement, and they are retiring faster than they can be replaced, the businesses lose the very thing that made them work.

We believe this is now solvable in a way it wasn't five years ago. Recent advances in AI mean that tacit knowledge (judgment, relationships, instinct) can be observed, mapped, and turned into something a team can actually use.

We are building a service that sits alongside the experts inside an SME, documents how the business actually runs today, suggests where it can run better, and turns the founder's and senior team's knowledge into something that scales beyond them.

### One-sentence version (for verbal intros)

Organically grown SMEs are running on expertise that was never written down, and as the people who hold it retire faster than they can be replaced, the businesses inherit a quiet operational debt that AI now lets us repay before it comes due.

## Thesis Evolution

### v1 — Starting point
"SME succession is emotional and operationally hard. Aging founders need to hand over their baby creature."

### v2 — Post-council
"SME succession is actually four distinct problems (matching, readiness, emotional, deal-structuring), and the framework is premature, fieldwork must come first."

### v3 — Post-first-interview
"The customer is the successor, not the founder. The job is to systemize a founder-centric business while still running it. Tacit knowledge extraction is the technical core."

## Key Assumptions (to validate)

1. Experts retire faster than they can be replaced → **needs data per sector**
2. Trapped knowledge in founders/seniors is now more reachable because of AI → **needs technical validation**
3. The successor is a better buyer than the founder → **needs 10+ interview confirmation**
4. Founders and senior employees will let themselves be shadowed → **biggest risk, untested**
5. Financial health of the business correlates with resistance to change → **emerging from interview 1**
6. SMEs will pay €5K–10K for a due diligence report → **first positive signal from Prisma founder, needs founder-side validation**
7. An internal champion (digital-responsible person) is required for engagement to stick → **strong signal from 200+ client experience**
